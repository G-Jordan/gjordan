Mission

G73 began on a Kalamazoo winter curb where breath looked like smoke signals and boots pressed poems into salt grit. I was raised where the train tracks hum under the skin and the furnace cough keeps time. Family trees offered fruit and fractures; we learned rhythm from survival — stretch a dollar, stitch a dream, hide the bottle, find the beam. The world felt calibrated for people with a head start, so I tuned my heart like a kick drum in the dark and promised to keep tempo for anybody who needed a count-in.

“If there is no struggle, there is no progress.” — Frederick Douglass

This site is the grown version of that promise. It’s a place where therapy becomes melody and software becomes leverage; where music meets tools, where art meets clarity, where a .music domain carries real weight for real people. The mission is simple: empower indie and underground artists with community and technology that respect them — no gatekeepers, no gimmicks, and no mystery math. We move like a band: drummer heart, bassline patience, melody hope, lyrics honest, arrangement kind. We make it loud enough to hear and clear enough to trust.

Mama kept our lights up with a will and a whisper. I kept a notebook thick with bars and blisters, collected verses from walking and wisdom from listening. I saw what the powder stole and what bad choices tried to tell me; I chose a remix over a rerun. If trauma laid the stems down crooked, I would still find a way to groove straight. I learned that curly braces could hold me tighter than worry, and that a clean layout could calm a loud mind. In the basement I shaped bass and snares; on the phone I shaped pages and prayers.

“For though the righteous fall seven times, they rise again.” — Proverbs 24:16

G73 runs on three fuels: dignity, discipline, and delight. Dignity means the artist owns their voice and understands their rights. Discipline means we ship with care — readable type, honest contrast, pages that load on prepaid data and older phones. Delight means the little things matter: chips that glow like badges you’ve earned, tabs that behave, backgrounds that breathe, words that land. We talk plainly about privacy and money because trust is part of the music.

There’s a specific heartbeat to this place: Kalamazoo generosity mixed with internet efficiency. Neighbors, cousins, choirs of porches — that chorus taught me to make space for people and put the mic where the shy kid can reach it. Our mission is not only to publish songs; it’s to publish courage, to publish clarity, to publish next steps. We keep the wall low and the welcome high. We build simple rails anyone can ride: upload, theme, share, measure, improve. The future brings tickets, merch, and split accounting, but the core stays the same — people over platforms, respect over rhetoric, craft over clout.

“Let your light shine before others, that they may see your good works and glorify your Father in heaven.” — Matthew 5:16

Every decision here is personal. If a feature doesn’t help an artist keep their promise to their listeners, it waits. If a sentence isn’t clear enough for a tired parent reading on a phone at a bus stop, we rewrite it. If a tab jumps you to the top when you didn’t ask, we fix the motion so your body and browser both feel respected. The work is built entirely on an Android phone because that’s how most of our audience lives — and that constraint keeps us disciplined, mobile-first, and human-scale. G73 is a bridge, not a maze. It’s a hand on your shoulder that says: take the next right loop. We’ll walk it with you.
